Projects during internship

Online experimentation in E-commerce with revenue and time constraints.

(Applied Scientist Intern, AWS, amazon) ALMC Reinforcement learning in E-commerce workshop, 2020   Jinjin Tian, Lenon Minorics, Guido Imbens  

Experimentation in E-commerce often have extra consideration of revenue constrain and time constrain. In this project we utilized recent advances in reinforcement learning and any-time valid inference to construct an online experimentation platform that allows efficient trading off between revenue constrain and time constrain in E-commerce. We also proposed new algorithm to deal with unknown abrupt changes over time, which only cost O(1) for each updates, and has much lower regret comparing with other state-of-arts.

Projects in undergraduate

Gaussian Graphical Structure Recovery via Knockoffs with Corrupted Data

Jinjin Tian, Jia Li, Zemin Zheng  

Learning the dependence structures in Gaussian graphical models is of fundamental importance in many contemporary applications. In this project, we proposed new procedure called CoCoKnockoff to recover the high dimensional gaussian graphical structure with FDR control given data with additive measurement error and unknown population covariance. With theoretical gauratees for FDR control, numerical studies show that CoCoKnockoff enjoy better statistical power compared with directly using the knockoff method.

Brain Task Classification Borrowing Strength from Region Connectivity Network Structure

Jinjin Tian, Jing Lei, Kehui Chen  

In this project, we demonstrated that the brain regions connectivity can be a good measure to classify the brain undertaking different tasks: using the brain structure to guide connections design between layers in deep learning model can significantly improve the classification accuracy and prevent overfitting. The results imply the potentials of the structure of network in classification of realtional data.
